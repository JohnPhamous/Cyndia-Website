2.008 Design & Manufacturing II

Poke-Yoyo

In 2.008, we learned about different manufacturing techniques used in industry and how to determine what process is the most appropriate in various situations. One of the most fun aspects of the class was getting parts that our staff cut open and trying to figure out how each component was made, and what the tooling used to make them looked like.

For the class project, every team designed a yo-yo and had to use a combination of injection molding and thermoforming to "mass produce" (make about 50) yo-yos. My team went the sweet and simple route, going for a sleek professional yo-yo style. Making the features on the sides relatively flat and having the majority of the features be circular made the molds very fast to machine and fix when necessary. As a result, this class was probably one of the least stressful classes I've ever taken at MIT.
