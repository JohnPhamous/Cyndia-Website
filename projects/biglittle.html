Pi Beta Phi Big Little Week

Fall 2015

Every fall, just before new members are initiated, they receive a big who is meant to guide them through their undergraduate experience. But before getting to all that serious stuff, we have a week of small gift-giving where bigs drop hints to their new littles about who they are to get them excited.

I'm really bad at giving hints, but I love making things for people, so I laser cut puzzle pieces inspired by Calvin and Hobbes, my favorite comic strip as a child. I picked my favorite individual images that I thought represented a great friendship (like dancing and adventure), traced them and did some basic image processing in MATLAB to get a consistent style, and finally laser cut the pieces with the help of my own big :)

I also really wanted to incorporate LEDs on her letters, and I diffused their light with tissue paper accents. If I were to do it again this year, I would probably paint a ombre dusk setting and use the LEDs to draw out constellations in the night sky - so if anyone is looking for an idea, there you go! I was all ready to solder and embed the LEDs in the foam core, but I found these awesome string lights (often referred to as fairy lights). I chose these for the super small, subtle battery casing - just beware that watch batteries don't last very long in this situation! However, there is a large variety of these out there, including some with remote control settings, so if you're looking for a daintier LED style, check those out.

Finally, as a fun joke, I flooded her room with these tissue paper pom poms... But as simple as they look, they take a LONG time to make, and it took me and my roommate nearly 2 hours to put all 20-25 up.
