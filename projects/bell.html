Bell Helicopter

Summer Internship 2015

This isn't a typical portfolio project... but I had a lot of fun with it so here it is.

I spent my sophomore year summer at Bell Helicopter in Fort Worth, TX. While I was spending my working hours with the Control Laws group, I thought the most interesting part of the experience was listening to Dr. Al Brand, the director of the Flight Technology group, teach us about the aerodynamics of how a helicopter flies. Coming into this, I had very little experience with aerodynamics - I just knew there was a little thing called drag that makes driving really fast a struggle for your car's gas mileage.

He showed us how blade flapping and feathering causes pressure differentials that allow the aircraft to tilt and turn. He also explained how the aerodynamics of hovering make it require significantly more power than forward flight, and he explained the crazy looking vortex rings that occur when tiltrotor aircrafts, like the V22, approach a landing. Basically I learned a lot of things I never even knew to question before that summer, so as a thank you to Al and to the rest of the engineers who guided us through the summer, David Shultz (another intern) and I made this poster for fun one Saturday afternoon. It was inspired by the Saturn 5 xkcd comic, which you should check out because it is one of my all time favorites.
